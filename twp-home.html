Preface

Welcome, you curious mind! Wait, question, did you clean that room yet? Take out the trash? Are you even organized? Well that aside, What you'll find here are some items that I've briefly cherished. I know it just looks like garbage! I know you, the reader, are wondering why these miscellaneous things are displayed here. To be honest, I wasn’t really interested in categorizing my recurring waste but see how I contribute to waste. Actually, I thought better yet, why not the unique objects? I say this in particular because of the phrase, “One can learn a lot from one’s trash,” and that got me thinking. What if the discarded, the overlooked, and seemingly useless had stories to tell? For example, a mangled concert wristband, a crumpled sticky note, or even an old worn-out car each carries a glimpse of the past often frozen in time.

So I started collecting, not just trash but the odd, rare things that sparked a significant memory. As trivial as that may sound, I found a worn-out lighter, or as I used to call it my lucky lighter, a small toy car that belonged to a kid who loves racing and fast cars. Who is that kid in question? Is he or she still interested in that specific topic?

Well, all that can be confusing but undoubtedly answered. Maybe trash isn’t just trash anymore. Maybe it's a unique breadcrumb trail that leads into our forgotten lives, abandoned dreams, and secret obsessions. Who knows but it does strike a renewed sense of curiosity. continue reading. . . .

Overview:

This collection of usual objects first begins with simple everyday items that were once useful but are now obsolete and deemed trash-worthy by time and convenience. Each object contains a slightly different tale of its past, discarded when the significance of its presence self becomes futile. But as I sifted through these forgotten remnants, I realized that trash isn't just about what’s thrown away; it's about what's left behind. “Some trash is recycled, some is thrown away, and some ends up where it shouldn’t end up” - Carlo Ritta These objects weren’t junk; some were finite and others unfinished stories, lost connections, and slivers of moments that once mattered. Over time, the collection grew its own chronicle, not just quantitatively speaking but also with quality and more life. What do we choose to keep and what do we let go of? With that being said, my objects contain significant pieces of my life that are spoken from the trash bin and the progression of the future.

Perhaps you too, are unaware of the lingering objects in some forgotten corner or bin. Maybe they are items that you once cherished but have outgrown. I suggest you take a moment to look around or even do some personal investigation of some sort; what stories you can find from your disregarded things? Who knows you just might unlock a core memory or something . . .

My items tell their own story but only one that matters to me. Fragments from my past attached to temporary objects others might see as worthless. Well without getting too philosophical, here’s what over the course of a few weeks, I gathered. . . .

Profile: Item of the week

Before

After

So my special item that I will be parting ways with is the glorious wrapping of my beloved Sweetish Fish. You see I have a deep somewhat intellectual bond with this candy, because not only does it satisfy my sweet tooth, but it also serves as my emotional support snack.

I usually purchase a bag or two from my nearest corner store, which runs me about a few dollars. As I study or paint I like to enjoy these chewy red-fish, subconsciously eating one at a time until I come to the realization that I am almost done.

And then I come the heartbreaking moment, as I stare at a nearly empty bag, I am forced to confront the cruel reality that my self-control is just as mythical as the nutrition in this candy.
